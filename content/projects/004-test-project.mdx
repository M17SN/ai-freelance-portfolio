## The Problem

CartPulse, a mid-size e-commerce company with ~$40M ARR, was losing customers every month
without any early warning. By the time the customer success team noticed, it was too late to intervene.
The Head of Customer Success needed a system that flagged at-risk customers before they churned —
and a way to present that to her team without requiring a data science background.

## The Data

Used the Telco Customer Churn dataset from Kaggle as a proxy for CartPulse's internal data.
Key features: tenure, monthly charges, contract type, payment method, support ticket history,
and service usage patterns. The dataset had ~7,000 rows with moderate class imbalance (~27% churn rate).

## Approach

### 1. Exploratory Data Analysis

- Identified tenure and contract type as the strongest churn signals
- Found that month-to-month customers with high monthly charges churned at 3x the rate of annual contract holders
- Handled missing values in TotalCharges (11 rows) caused by zero-tenure customers

### 2. Feature Engineering

- Created `charges_per_tenure` ratio to capture value perception
- Binned tenure into cohorts (new, growing, mature, loyal)
- One-hot encoded categorical features

### 3. Model Selection

Tested Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost.
XGBoost won on AUC-ROC (0.847) and was chosen for its speed and explainability support.
Addressed class imbalance using `scale_pos_weight`.

### 4. Explainability

Integrated SHAP values to explain individual predictions — critical for the CS team
to understand _why_ a customer is flagged, not just _that_ they are.

## Results

| Metric    | Score |
| --------- | ----- |
| AUC-ROC   | 0.847 |
| Precision | 0.72  |
| Recall    | 0.68  |
| F1 Score  | 0.70  |

Top churn drivers: month-to-month contract, high monthly charges, no tech support, short tenure.

## Deliverables

- Production-ready XGBoost model with preprocessing pipeline
- SHAP explainability layer per customer
- CSV risk report: every customer scored with churn probability and top 3 risk factors
- Plain-English executive summary for the VP
